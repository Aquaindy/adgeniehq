1What Outreach is — /outreach

Outreach is your backlink-building workspace. You collect the sites you'd like a link from (prospects), draft a tailored email to each contact, and work them from first touch to a live backlink. It is deliberately human-in-the-loop: the AI (or a template) writes the draft, but a person approves and sends.

  • Prospects list at /outreach — every domain you're tracking, with status, contact, relevance and domain-authority scores.
  • Prospect detail at /outreach/:id — edit the contact, draft emails, and run the approve → send → reply flow.
  • A usage meter tracks outreach-email sends against your plan limit; sending is blocked once you hit the cap.

✅ You approve every send

No email in this module reaches a recipient without an Admin approving it first. Drafts, edits, and follow-ups all wait for that human step by design.

2Backlink prospects — discover, add & score

A prospect is one domain you'd like a backlink from. There are three ways to add them:

MethodWhat it doesAccess
Add manuallyEnter a domain plus an optional contact name/email, right on the Outreach page.Member
Discover from competitorCrawl a competitor URL, collect the external sites it links to, and add each new domain (scored by mention count). New domains only — existing rows are never overwritten.Marketer+
Bulk importPaste a CSV-style batch of domains/contacts. Per-row errors (duplicate or invalid domain) are reported without aborting the whole batch.Marketer+

Each prospect can carry a relevance score, a domain authority (DA), a specific target page, contact role, and free-text notes. Domains are normalized (scheme, www., and casing stripped) and are unique per workspace — you can't add the same site twice.

3Drafting outreach emails — personalized per prospect

Open a prospect and use Draft an outreach email. The draft is personalized from that prospect and your Growth DNA / onboarding profile.

  1. Add a contact email first — you can't draft to a prospect with no contact_email. Edit the contact card to add one.
  2. Optionally set an angle and sender name — a hook for the pitch and the name it should sign off with.
  3. Draft email — uses your configured LLM if available, otherwise a deterministic template, so you always get a real draft. It lands in draft status.
  4. Edit inline — tweak the subject and body while it's a draft. (Editing an approved email drops it back to draft so it must be re-approved.)

Every draft records its source (agent / deterministic / follow-up) and the model used, and each draft is audit-logged. You can draft more than one email per prospect — they're listed newest-first on the prospect page.

4Approval before send — nothing sends on its own

This is the safety core of the module. A draft becomes a real email only after an Admin approves it, and only then can it be sent.

  1. Approve — an Admin+ reviews the draft and approves it. Status → approved, stamped with who approved and when.
  2. Send via SMTP — an Admin+ sends. SMTP can hang for 10+ seconds, so with workers enabled the send runs in the background and you get immediate feedback.
  3. Status flips — on success → sent (and the prospect → contacted, timestamped). If SMTP isn't configured or the transport rejects it → failed with the error saved, so a message is never silently dropped.
  4. Retry or track — a failed email can be re-sent. A sent email can be marked replied or won by hand if you're not using inbound tracking.

Guardrails on send

Sends are throttled per minute to protect your sending domain, and counted against your plan's outreach-email limit. Every send appends a CAN-SPAM/GDPR unsubscribe footer automatically, and each send/approve/edit is written to the audit log.

Reply tokens & threading

The first time an email goes out, AdGenieHQ mints a signed reply token and sends the message with a Reply-To of reply+<token>@<your-inbound-domain>. When the recipient replies, your inbound-email parse service forwards it to AdGenieHQ, which matches the token and:

  • marks the email replied and the prospect replied — or
  • if the message looks like a bounce, marks the email bounced and the prospect bounced.

The token is HMAC-signed to the deployment secret, so forged or tampered tokens are rejected before they ever touch your data — and one conversation always routes back to the same email row for clean threading.

5Auto follow-ups — drafted, never auto-sent

Prospects go quiet. A background job (Celery beat) finds sent emails that have had no reply past your configured silence threshold and no existing follow-up, then drafts a gentle bump for each.

  • The follow-up is a draft — an Admin still has to approve it before it can send. The auto-scheduler only writes drafts; it never sends.
  • The subject prepends Re: and the body opens with a short "bumping this to the top of your inbox," then quotes the original note for context.
  • You can also draft a follow-up on demand from any sent (or replied) email (Marketer+). Follow-ups chain to the original via parent_email_id with an incrementing step_index.

✅ Still human-gated

Auto follow-ups exist to save you writing time, not to take over your inbox. Like every other email here, they wait in draft for an Admin to approve.

?FAQ & tips

Can it email people without me?

No. Every outreach email — first touch or follow-up — is created as a draft. It can only be sent after an Admin approves it and an Admin triggers the send. There is no mode that sends automatically.

What happens when a prospect replies?

If you've set up inbound-email routing, the reply lands at the message's reply+<token> address, AdGenieHQ matches the token, and it marks the email and prospect replied automatically (or bounced if it's a bounce notice). No inbound setup? Just click Mark replied or Mark won on the sent email yourself.

Why can't I draft an email for this prospect?

The prospect needs a contact email. Open the contact card, add one, and the draft form appears.

My send said "failed" — what now?

Almost always SMTP isn't configured (set SMTP_HOST and friends) or the transport rejected the message. The exact error is saved on the email; fix the config and hit Send again to retry.

Do I need an LLM key to draft?

No. Without a key, drafting falls back to a deterministic template so you still get a real, personalized email. With a key, the copy gets richer.

Who can do what?

Adding and drafting are open to any member; discover, bulk import, editing emails, follow-ups and marking replies need Marketer+; approving and sending require Admin+.
