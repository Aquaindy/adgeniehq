1What it is

The Studio is where the AI drafts your written content and organic social — and where you stay firmly in charge of it. Two surfaces, one draft store:

SurfaceRouteWhat it does
Content Studio/contentGenerate, edit, approve, and publish long- and short-form copy: blog posts, landing-page copy, ad copy, meta descriptions, emails, social posts.
Social Studio/socialTurn one topic (or a web link) into a platform-native draft per network, plus Reels/Shorts/TikTok video scripts — each with its own keywords, hashtags, and image.
  • Drafts are grounded in your Growth DNA and onboarding — offer, audience, brand voice — so output sounds like your business, not a generic template.
  • Every generation is saved and traceable: each draft links back to the agent run that produced it, and the model used (or deterministic when no LLM was used).
  • You are the editor. The AI proposes; you edit, approve, reject, or archive. Publishing is always a deliberate human step.

3Generate & edit a draft

On /content, use the Generate a draft form. The agent uses your configured LLM if one is set; otherwise it falls back to a deterministic template populated from onboarding — so you always get a real draft.

  1. Pick a type — blog post, landing page, ad copy, meta description, email, or social post.
  2. Give it a topic (required), and optionally an audience and comma-separated keywords.
  3. Generate — the new draft appears in the list with its type, source, and model badges.
  4. Open it to read the full body, then Edit the title and body inline (available until it's published or archived).

Extra tools on a draft:

  • AI Assist (Marketer+) — one-shot actions on a draft: outline, expand, refine, suggest_title, suggest_meta. It returns a suggestion; you decide to insert, replace, or discard it. Token and dollar cost are metered.
  • Refresh / repurpose — rewrite an existing draft, or turn any URL into a new draft (the origin is recorded on the new draft).
  • Export — download a draft as .txt or .docx (the .docx embeds its image), or export all drafts to CSV.
  • Manual drafts — you can also hand-author a draft via the API; it lands in the same review flow.

✅ Nothing ships by itself

Drafts are never auto-published. Publishing is a deliberate step behind an Admin approval — and for networks without a publishing integration it's a manual "mark published" that records where you posted it. AdGenieHQ does not post to social networks for you.

5Social Studio — /social

One topic in, a tailored draft per platform out — each with the right length, tone, and hashtag convention for that network.

  1. Start from a topic or paste a web link to repurpose (we fetch the page and turn its content into posts; leave the topic blank to use the page's own title).
  2. Optional angle — audience, a link to promote, and a call to action.
  3. Pick platforms across Posts and Reels & Shorts. Each card shows its length and hashtag limits before you generate.
  4. Generate — one draft per selected platform, each saved to /content. Copy for posting, download the whole pack as .docx/.txt, or open any draft to refine it.

Posts

  • LinkedIn — up to 3,000 chars, 3–5 hashtags
  • Facebook — long-form, few/no hashtags
  • X (Twitter) — hard 280-char ceiling, 0–2 hashtags
  • Instagram — up to 2,200 chars, 8–15 hashtags
  • Pinterest — search-intent pin description
  • Threads — casual, 0–1 hashtag

Reels & Shorts (scripts)

  • TikTok — 9:16, ~21–60s
  • Instagram Reels — 9:16, ~15–60s
  • YouTube Shorts — 9:16, ~20–60s

Each script is shot-by-shot: a Hook (0–2s), numbered scene beats (narration + on-screen text + visual), and a closing CTA, with the target aspect ratio and duration.

  • Each network draft carries its own keywords and hashtags. Posts show a live composed character count (body + hashtags) against the platform limit, and flag anything that would go over once hashtags are appended.
  • Provenance is honest: drafts made with an LLM show the model; those built from the deterministic skeleton are badged deterministic.
  • Generating a pack needs Marketer+ and spends one content-draft credit per platform.

6The generated image

Social drafts can get a matching AI creative image, saved onto the draft. Pick a style, then Generate image (or regenerate later):

StyleWhat it renders
ConceptHeadline + CTA + graphics — a clean, scroll-stopping promo card
Product coverA 3D product-box mockup with the headline and CTA

📌 What text goes on the image

The headline rendered onto the image is the draft's title — not the shared hook or the body. The detail page shows it back to you as "Text sent to the image: …". Want different image text? Edit the draft title first, then generate.

Image generation requires an OpenAI key and Marketer+, and costs image credits. In a social pack you can also generate images for every draft at once (run serially so the credit spend stays legible).

7Where content flows next

  • Blog — blog_post drafts feed the Blog module (/blog/posts); once approved and published they power your public blog with slug, excerpt, and cover image.
  • Campaigns & Creatives — ad_copy and the copy the Studio generates surface alongside the Creatives library (/creatives), ready to attach to ad sets and campaigns.
  • Reports — saved drafts and agent runs are real outputs your reports can summarize; no data means no fabricated numbers.

Because every draft is a saved, workspace-scoped record tied to its agent run, it's traceable end to end — from the run that wrote it to wherever you take it next.

8Plan limits & AI credits

  • Content generation is metered. Both pages show a usage meter for the content_drafts resource so you can see where you stand on your plan.
  • A social pack costs one content-draft credit per platform — select three platforms, spend three.
  • Image generation costs image credits and needs an OpenAI key; AI Assist calls are metered against your workspace's LLM meter with token and dollar cost recorded.
  • Editing, approving, publishing, exporting, and copying an existing draft do not spend generation credits.

?FAQ & tips

Does anything auto-post to my social accounts?

No. Everything is created as a draft, an Admin must approve it, and publishing is a deliberate step — for social there's no auto-poster, so you copy the finished text (and download the image) and post it yourself. AdGenieHQ never posts on your behalf.

What image is shown, and what text is on it?

The image is generated per social draft in the style you pick (Concept or Product cover), and the headline burned onto it is the draft's title. Change the title before generating to change the on-image text.

Do I need an LLM / OpenAI key?

Not to write copy — every agent has a deterministic fallback that builds a real draft from your onboarding, badged deterministic. With a key, drafts get richer. Image generation does require an OpenAI key.

Can I turn an existing blog post into social posts?

Yes. In the Social Studio, paste the URL under "repurpose a web link" — we fetch the page and spin it into a post or script per platform. Leave the topic blank to use the page's own title, or set both to angle it.

Why is a draft flagged as over the limit?

Platforms count the body plus hashtags together. The composed character count is shown against each platform's ceiling (e.g. X's 280); trim the body before posting if it's over.

Who can generate, approve, and publish?

Generating a social pack, generating images, and AI Assist need Marketer or above. Approving and publishing are Admin-gated. Viewers and analysts can read drafts but not spend credits or ship them.
