⚡ 60-second quickstart

Open Autoresponders → pick a provider card → paste your API key (Admin role) → Connect → choose a list or tag → paste contacts and Push to list, or Pull from list. Check the Recent contact syncs feed. Done.

1What it is

The Autoresponders module connects AdGenieHQ to your email/SMS marketing platform so your audiences flow between the two systems:

  • Push — send contacts (e.g. leads captured in AdGenieHQ) into a list, tag, or segment on your provider.
  • Pull — read subscribers from a provider list back into AdGenieHQ.

Each provider plugs in through a small adapter, so adding platforms doesn't change how you use the page. Credentials are encrypted at rest and never returned to the browser; the platform only talks to your provider when you explicitly connect, list, push, or pull.

2Supported providers

Three connectors ship today. The first two are first-class adapters; the third covers everything else.

Contacts pull

Omnisend and GetResponse both support pulling contacts back. The Custom connector can pull only if you configure a Pull URL — otherwise it's push-only.

3Connecting a provider — /autoresponders

Open Autoresponders in the sidebar. Each provider shows as a card with a status pill. Connecting requires the workspace Admin role.

  1. Enter credentials — the API key (Omnisend / GetResponse) or the Push/Pull URLs and optional auth token (Custom).
  2. Connect — AdGenieHQ verifies the credentials with the provider before saving them. Bad keys are rejected and the card flips to an error state with the reason.
  3. Stored securely — on success the key is Fernet-encrypted at rest; non-secret settings (URLs, header names) are saved as plain config. The card shows the connected account name.
  4. Disconnect anytime (Admin) — the secret is dropped immediately, the card returns to disconnected, and the row is kept for history.

Connection status is one of three, shown on the pill: connected · disconnected · error. Any last error from the provider is surfaced right on the card.

4Syncing audiences

Once connected, the card opens a Sync contacts panel. Pushing and pulling requires the Marketer role or higher.

Choose the audience

  • GetResponse — pick a list from the dropdown (fetched live from your account).
  • Omnisend — type a free-text tag; pushed contacts are tagged so your segments/automations catch them.
  • Custom — type a free-text list value that's forwarded to your endpoint.

Push contacts

Paste contacts one per line as email, first name, last name (name parts optional), then Push to list. GetResponse requires an email; Omnisend and Custom accept email or phone.

Pull contacts

Pull from list reads up to the configured limit of subscribers back into the page for review. AdGenieHQ stores only the counts of a pull, not the contact PII.

FieldWhat it captures
Directionpush (AdGenieHQ → provider) or pull (provider → AdGenieHQ)

Outcome rules: all succeed → succeeded; some fail → partial; none succeed → failed. Provider error messages are captured on the sync record.

5Health & audit

Every connection and sync is traceable — nothing happens silently.

  • Connection health at a glance — each card shows its status pill, the connected account name, the last sync time, and any last provider error.
  • Recent contact syncs feed — a running ledger of every push and pull with direction, counts, target audience, status, and timestamp. Empty until your first sync.
  • Full audit logging — connect, connect-failed, disconnect, and every push/pull are written to the workspace audit log with the actor, IP address, and user agent.
  • Least data retained — pulled contacts are shown for review but only their counts are persisted; secrets are dropped on disconnect.

✅ Safe by default

No contact leaves AdGenieHQ without an authorized connection and an explicit push you trigger. Credentials are verified before they're stored, encrypted while stored, and never exposed to the frontend.

6How it feeds Email Marketing

Autoresponders is the connection layer; the Email Marketing module is where you analyze what those platforms send.

  • Connect Omnisend here first, then open the Email module and click Sync now to pull your email campaigns with engagement and deliverability metrics.
  • Contacts pushed here (tagged in Omnisend) can be targeted by the automations and journeys you monitor elsewhere in AdGenieHQ.

?FAQ & tips

My platform isn't listed — Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, Klaviyo, Brevo…

Use the Custom / Webhook connector. Point its Push URL (and optional Pull URL) at your platform's contact endpoint, add an auth header if needed, and it works like any other provider — no waiting for a first-class adapter.

Are my API keys safe?

Yes. Keys are encrypted at rest with Fernet, are never returned to the browser or written to logs, and are deleted the moment you disconnect. They're also verified with the provider before being saved, so a bad key never becomes a "working" connection.

Why can't I pick a list for Omnisend?

Omnisend has no public endpoint to enumerate lists, so you push to a free-text tag instead. Your Omnisend segments and automations target that tag.

Who can connect and who can sync?

Admin role connects and disconnects providers. Marketer role (or higher) can push and pull contacts.

Does connecting send anything automatically?

No. Connecting only verifies the credentials. Contacts move only when you explicitly push or pull — and each of those is recorded in the sync feed and the audit log.
