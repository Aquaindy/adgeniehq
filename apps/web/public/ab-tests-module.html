1What A/B testing is

An A/B test compares two or more variants of the same thing — an ad or a landing page — and measures which one wins on a chosen objective. AdGenieHQ splits traffic between variants, counts what happens, and tells you when the difference is real rather than noise.

  • Variants — at least two, each with a unique name; one may be marked the control (your baseline).
  • Traffic share — the slice of visitors each variant receives. Shares must sum to 100% (leave them blank and they split evenly).
  • Objective — what "winning" means: conversion_rate, click_through_rate, signup_rate, revenue_per_visit, plus cpa and roas for ad tests.
  • Hypothesis (optional) — a one-line note on what you expect and why, saved with the test.

Real data only

Results are counted from tracked exposures (a visitor saw a variant) and conversions (they acted). AdGenieHQ never fabricates visits or conversion rates — an unlaunched or untracked test simply shows dashes.

3Create a test — /ab-tests

Open A/B tests and use the Create a test card. Choose the Landing page or Ad creative tab, name the test, pick an objective, and fill in the two starter variants.

  1. Name & objective — give the test a clear name and choose what winning means.
  2. Landing page: paste the control URL and the treatment URL.
    Ad creative: pick the provider, enter the account ID, and set each variant's campaign name + daily budget (in cents).
  3. Generate variants with AI (optional) — the variant generator drafts 1–4 new variants from your control, grounded in your onboarding offer + brand voice. No LLM key? It falls back to a deterministic skeleton you edit before shipping.
  4. Save — the test is created in ready; traffic shares auto-rebalance to sum to 100%.

Guardrails at create time: at least two variants, unique non-empty names, at most one control, and shares that total 1.0. AI variants can only be added while the test is draft or ready — not after launch.

Creating a test, recording metrics, and archiving are Marketer+ actions.

4Launch safely

Launching is an Admin+ action — it's the gate that can put campaigns on a provider, so it's never automatic.

TargetWhat "Launch" doesSpend risk
Landing pageFlips the test to launched and timestamps variants. Assignment + conversion tracking begins.None — no ad money involved.
Ad creativeCreates one campaign per variant via the provider write pipeline, each paused. Runs as a background task; each variant gets its own provider id.Paused — nothing spends until you activate it.

🛡️ Why your spend is safe

Ad variants publish paused by default. To start spending you flip a campaign to active in the provider's UI — or through an approve-then-execute recommendation in AdGenieHQ. Money never moves on launch alone.

Partial launches

If a provider rejects some variants, the ones that launched stay launched and the test stays ready so you can retry just the failures — AdGenieHQ won't roll back a partial spend. If every variant fails, launch surfaces the provider error instead of a hollow "launched" test.

5Traffic strategy (bandit)

For landing-page tests, the bandit strategy (set when the test is created) decides how visitors are assigned to variants. Assignment is sticky — a visitor keeps the same variant on every return.

StrategyHow traffic is splitBest for
Static staticFixed weights — each variant gets exactly its traffic share, start to finish.Clean, even splits and classic significance testing.
Thompson sampling thompson_samplingSamples a Beta posterior from live visits + conversions on every assignment, funnelling more traffic to the leading variant while still exploring the others.Cutting losses early — shifting traffic toward the winner as evidence builds.

Thompson sampling naturally balances exploit vs explore: an under-tested variant has a wide posterior, so it still gets occasional traffic instead of being starved.

6Measuring & the winner

Where the numbers come from

  • Public snippet — drop adgeniehq-ab.js on your landing page (redirect mode for separate URLs, or in-place mode to swap copy on one URL). It sticky-assigns a variant and records an exposure; call adgeniehqConvert() on a form submit / purchase / signup to record a conversion.
  • GA4 sync — pull sessions + conversions from a connected Google Analytics property, matched to each variant's URL path.
  • Manual metrics — for ad tests (or off-platform data), open a variant and enter visits + conversions yourself.

Each variant shows visits, conversions, and conversion rate. Landing-page counts refresh live from the tracked events — no sync job needed.

Declaring a winner

AdGenieHQ runs a two-proportion z-test (95% confidence by default) with confidence intervals and a suggested winner. Declaring a winner is an Admin+ action and, for landing-page tests, is guarded:

  • Underpowered — refused until each variant clears the minimum sample size.
  • Not significant — refused while the p-value is above the threshold. Keep the test running.
  • Override — pass force to declare anyway (e.g. you have qualitative, off-platform proof).

Declaring a winner marks the test completed and stamps its end time. Ad tests rely on your entered metrics, so their winner isn't blocked by the significance guard.

?FAQ & tips

Is my ad spend safe when I launch an ad test?

Yes. Launching is Admin-gated, and every ad variant is published paused. No budget spends until you explicitly activate a campaign — in the provider's UI or via an approved recommendation. Results, in turn, always come from real tracked data, never simulated numbers.

How is the winner chosen?

A two-proportion z-test at 95% confidence, plus a minimum-sample check. For landing-page tests you can't declare a winner while the result is underpowered or not statistically significant — unless you override with force. AdGenieHQ also suggests the leading variant for you.

Do I need a tracking snippet?

For landing-page tests, yes — that's how exposures and conversions are counted. Install the redirect or in-place snippet, and fire adgeniehqConvert() on your success event. Prefer analytics? Sync visits + conversions from a connected GA4 property instead.

Can AI write my variants?

Yes. The variant generator drafts 1–4 alternatives from your control and your onboarding offer + brand voice. With no LLM key it produces an editable skeleton so you always get a real starting point. You can only generate while the test is draft or ready.

Static or Thompson sampling — which split?

Use static for a clean, even test and textbook significance. Use Thompson sampling when you'd rather shift traffic toward the leader as evidence accumulates and cut wasted exposure to a losing variant.
