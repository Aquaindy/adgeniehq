⚡ 60-second quickstart

Open /blog/posts → New post → write the title & body → set the slug in Settings (optional) → Approve (Admin) → Publish. It appears on your blog and/or is pushed to your own site. Done.

1What the Blog module is — /blog/posts

The Blog module turns long-form drafts into published pages with proper SEO structure — a slug, excerpt, meta tags, keywords, and a cover image. Each post is a content_draft of type blog_post, so the same review-and-approve discipline applies as everywhere else in AdGenieHQ.

  • Long-form → live pages. Compose in Markdown, preview exactly what readers see at /blog/{slug}, then publish.
  • Grounded, not generic. AI help draws on your workspace context and offer so posts match your business, not a template. Topic ideas can flow in from your SEO & GEO keyword work.
  • Honest by default. Disconnected/empty? You get a clean empty state and a New post button — never placeholder articles or invented metrics.

The list at /blog/posts shows every blog_post draft in the workspace — ones you start here plus any generated elsewhere in AdGenieHQ (e.g. the Content Writer / Content & Social studio). Filter by status and click any post to open its editor.

2Writing & editing — the 3-tab editor

New post mints an empty draft and drops you straight into the editor at /blog/posts/:id. Each post has three tabs:

TabWhat you do there
EditorTitle + Markdown body with a live preview. Drag an image into the body (or Insert image) to upload & embed it. Pick a cover image.
AI AssistantOutline, expand a selection, refine a selection, suggest titles, suggest meta. Each result is a card you Insert / Replace / Discard — you stay in control.
SettingsSlug, excerpt, target URL, keywords, cover image, and SEO metadata (meta title, meta description, OG image, canonical URL).
  1. Write — type the title and body. Markdown is supported and the preview updates as you go.
  2. Add media — drop an image into the body to upload + insert it, and set a cover image for cards and the post header.
  3. Ask the assistant (optional) — pick an action; keep only what you like via Append / Use as title / Apply to settings.
  4. Save — persists your edits. Each post counts against your plan's content-draft limit.

✅ No LLM key? Still fully usable.

The editor is 100% manual — you never need AI to write. When no LLM is configured, the AI Assistant returns a deterministic stub clearly tagged "stub (no LLM key)" instead of a hallucination. With a key, suggestions get richer and are metered against your workspace's LLM budget.

3Status workflow — draft → published

Every post moves through the same lifecycle, and each transition is written to the audit log with actor, IP, and user agent.

StatusMeaningWho can move it
draftBeing written. Editable.Edit: Marketer+
approvedSigned off, ready to go live. Still not public.Approve: Admin+
publishedLive. Slug + excerpt auto-fill if they were blank.Publish: Admin+
archivedRetired from the working list.Archive: Marketer+
rejectedSent back (from draft or approved).Reject: Marketer+
  • You can only edit a post while it's draft, approved, or rejected — published and archived posts are locked.
  • Only an approved draft can be published, so nothing skips review.
  • The /blog/posts list shows a status pill on every row and a status filter up top.

4Publishing & the webhook

Publishing is always a deliberate, human Admin action on an approved draft. On publish, AdGenieHQ auto-fills a slug (from the title) and an excerpt (first part of the body) if you left them blank, so the post is render-ready. There are three ways a published post reaches readers:

DestinationHow it worksSetup
AdGenieHQ public blogServed at /public/blog and /public/blog/{slug}. Only the workspace configured as the marketing workspace surfaces here; otherwise the endpoints stay empty (honest empty state, never fabricated).MARKETING_WORKSPACE_SLUG env
Your own site (publish webhook)AdGenieHQ POSTs the draft as JSON to your URL with the secret in Authorization: Bearer …. Your endpoint returns {"published_url": "https://…"}, which we store on the post.Workspace Settings → Publish webhook (Admin)
Manual URLPublish elsewhere yourself and record the live URL on the post.None

The webhook contract is generic — WordPress, Webflow, Ghost, Contentful, a Zapier/Make hook, or a small custom adapter all just implement the same JSON POST. An Admin sets publish_webhook_url and a secret (stored encrypted) at /workspaces/{id}/publish-webhook; the full contract lives in docs/cms-publish-webhook.md.

Heads up — the webhook must confirm

If the webhook is configured, the post is only marked published after your receiver responds successfully. A failure surfaces as an error you can retry once your endpoint is healthy — the draft won't silently flip to published.

5SEO & structure

Good structure is what makes a post discoverable in both classic search and AI answer engines. The Settings tab covers all of it:

FieldUsed for
SlugThe URL at /blog/{slug}. Unique per workspace; auto-generated from the title on publish if blank.
ExcerptCards, OG previews, and RSS. Auto-derived from the body on publish if blank.
KeywordsSearch targeting for the post (distinct from social hashtags).
Meta title / descriptionThe <head> tags. Keep the title ≤60 and description ≤155 characters.
OG image / canonical URLSocial share cards and canonicalization for duplicate content.
Cover imageShown on listing cards and the post header.

This ties directly into the SEO & GEO agent: its keyword opportunities and content gaps are the raw material for what to write, and the meta/slug/excerpt you set here are what its audits look for. Write the post, wire up the metadata, and the SEO module can track how it performs.

?FAQ & tips

Where do published posts appear?

Depends on your setup. If this workspace is the configured marketing workspace, published posts show on the AdGenieHQ public blog at /public/blog. Otherwise they're meant for your own site via the publish webhook. Either way, the /blog/posts list always shows every post and its live status.

How does my own site get the post?

Through the publish webhook. On publish, AdGenieHQ sends the draft as JSON to the URL you configured (with a bearer secret), and your endpoint replies with the published_url it created. Configure it once at workspace Settings → Publish webhook — it works with WordPress, Webflow, Ghost, Contentful, Zapier/Make, or a custom adapter.

Can I schedule posts to publish later?

No — there's no scheduled or automatic publishing by design. Publishing is a manual Admin action on an approved draft, so a human always makes the call. Prepare the post, approve it, and publish when you're ready.

Do I need AI to write a post?

No. The editor is fully manual. The AI Assistant is optional, and without an LLM key it returns clearly tagged deterministic stubs rather than making things up.

What if I leave the slug or excerpt blank?

They're auto-filled on publish — the slug from the title (made unique), the excerpt from the start of the body. You can set either explicitly in Settings beforehand to control them.
